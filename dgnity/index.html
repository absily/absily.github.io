Ensuring Dignity and Rights for Persons with Disabilities and the Elderly: A Call for Transparent and Respectful Care
عربي

While browsing the social media page of the Ministry of Social Affairs, a post about the distribution of diapers to people with disabilities and the elderly caught my attention. These posts have profound implications for me as a citizen committed to human rights and the dignity of all people, particularly the rights of persons with disabilities.

Providing care and support to these groups is not merely an act of charity; it is a humanitarian and legal duty established by both international and domestic laws. The state must commit to delivering comprehensive care to these individuals and upholding their fundamental rights with dignity and respect.

The government must adopt complete transparency in the distribution processes and ensure that aid reaches all those entitled to it without discrimination or bias. This requires serious efforts to achieve social justice and respect for human rights for all.

We must strongly criticize the way this initiative is promoted on the Ministry of Social Affairs' social media pages. Highlighting and promoting these initiatives prominently on social media gives the impression that the ministry is performing charitable acts worthy of praise, whereas in reality, it is merely fulfilling its most basic obligations to its citizens.

The rights of persons with disabilities, the incapacitated, and the elderly must be respected and implemented quietly and with dignity, not through promotional campaigns aimed at achieving political or social gains. It is essential to have a clear legal framework that the government adheres to, ensuring the provision of comprehensive and sustainable care to these groups. This includes providing healthcare, educational, and social services that enable them to live with dignity and independence within the community.
