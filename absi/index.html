About Me

Hello! I'm Abdusalam Mustafa Shlebak, a passionate disability rights advocate, blogger, and a person living with polio. I'm committed to breaking down barriers and promoting inclusivity for everyone.

Early Life and Challenges

I was born in Janzour, Libya, on October 23rd, 1971.

At six months old, I contracted polio. My early years involved extensive travel for treatment, including a significant time in Belgrade, Serbia.

Eventually, I returned to Libya, but the lack of accessible infrastructure and societal challenges hindered my ability to attend school. I persevered by learning through television programs and with the unwavering support of my family.

Embracing Technology

My journey into the world of technology began with exploring websites like BBC, Arabia.com, and Yahoo. I designed my first website, "Janzour My Home," in 2004.

I also supervised Libyan forums and later embraced social media platforms like YouTube, where I shared videos about my experiences.

Technology became a powerful tool, enabling me to connect with others and share my story.

Contributions and Advocacy

I designed the first official website for Tripoli’s club for people with disabilities in 2007. I also joined the Libyan Paralympics and Special Libyan Olympics media offices.

This work allowed me to contribute to raising awareness and promoting the achievements of people with disabilities.

Blogging and Personal Message

I started blogging in 2009 at absily.wordpress.com. My blog focuses on disability rights and my personal experiences, aiming to raise awareness and inspire others.

"My motivation in life is ‘to be or not to be’ and ‘Live Strong.’"

I share my story in the hope of helping others facing similar challenges. Technology has truly transformed my life, giving me a voice and enabling me to reach a global audience.
